What you’re seeing in a shoreline stone

Natural variation is not a flaw — it’s the point.

Surface texture

Tiny pits, faint scratches, and subtle unevenness can appear because these stones formed in nature and spent time in water and sand. Light cleaning preserves character without hiding reality.

Colour shifts in different light

Indoor lighting can mute warmth or deepen greens. Daylight often shows more detail and softer gradients. If you want a daylight photo, it’s always reasonable to request one.

Veins and lines

Lighter “veins” can be mineral-filled cracks (often quartz or similar). These are common in natural stone and can be one of the most striking features.

Shape and edges

Some pieces are naturally smooth; others have sharper edges. Each listing is chosen to match its purpose: display, handling, or future jewelry potential.

Care and cleaning

Simple care keeps the surface looking its best.

Everyday care

  • Wipe gently: Use a soft cloth to remove fingerprints or dust.
  • Avoid chemicals: Household cleaners can dull surfaces over time.
  • Store thoughtfully: Keep pieces separate if they can scratch one another.

Water and soaking

  • Short rinse is fine: Lukewarm water is usually safe for a quick rinse.
  • Avoid long soaking: Especially for stones with seams, cracks, or softer minerals.
  • Dry fully: Pat dry and allow air-drying before storing.

Oils and shine

  • Less is more: Oils can temporarily deepen colour, but they attract dust.
  • Natural finish: The goal is a clean, honest surface—never an artificial gloss.
  • If you’re unsure: Ask before applying anything to a piece you plan to keep long-term.

Custom pieces

How we decide what’s suitable for jewelry.

Selection criteria

For jewelry, shape matters. I look for stable structure, a comfortable profile, and natural features that will present well in a pendant setting.

Hardware and materials

Findings and chains are selected for durability and comfort. If a listing notes a pendant and chain, that means it’s ready as-worn—not a separate add-on.

What “custom” can mean

Custom can be as simple as choosing a chain length, or as detailed as selecting a setting style. The goal is always a finished piece that feels intentional and wearable.

Timeline expectations

Custom work takes time to do properly. If you are ordering as a gift, mention your date and we’ll choose a realistic option.
