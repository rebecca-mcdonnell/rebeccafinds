Education

Natural shoreline stone is defined by variation, texture, and mineral structure. This guide explains what to expect and how to care for each piece.

Natural variation Care & handling Custom options
Core principle

Stones are presented as natural materials — not factory-perfect objects. Mineral lines, surface texture, and colour shifts are part of what makes each piece one-of-one.

Listings prioritize clarity and accuracy.

What natural stone can show

Features commonly seen in shoreline finds.

Texture and tiny pits

Shoreline wear can create small pits, faint abrasions, or matte zones. These are normal in natural stone.

Veins and mineral lines

Lighter lines are often mineral-filled seams (commonly quartz or similar). These can add strong contrast.

Colour shifts by light

Greens can appear deeper indoors and brighter in daylight. Warm tones can intensify under sun.

Natural structure

Small inclusions and subtle seams are part of formation and history. Each piece is assessed before listing.

Care and handling

Simple steps for long-lasting clarity.

Everyday care

  • Wipe gently: Soft cloth for dust and fingerprints.
  • Avoid chemicals: Household cleaners can dull surfaces.
  • Store separately: Prevent stones from scratching one another.

Water guidance

  • Quick rinse: Lukewarm water is generally safe.
  • No long soaking: Especially for stones with seams or softer structure.
  • Dry fully: Pat dry and air-dry before storage.

Finish and shine

  • Natural finish: Presented clean, not artificially glossy.
  • Oils: Can attract dust and change surface feel.
  • Best practice: Keep surfaces dry and handled gently.
